AUGMENTED HISTORY

We have a beautiful city. There are many notable historic landmarks throughout, from the mighty Mississippi to the hills of Prospect Park. From the early milling and railroad days, to its relatively bland, but stable food sources. We also have a lot of stories, some that may stretch the truth at times. I'm looking at you, Paul Bunyan. As we become separated from past historical events, I imagine a layer of technology acting as a connective tissue to bring them forward into a new age. Using our ability to tell great stories, we will combine a new era of tall tales with our ubiquitous digital devices, innovating as we have always done in the Land of Lakes, connecting people back to the history they never were aware of. And right now we need a laugh. And a way to get out into our community and connect in a contactless way. The timing is right for Augmented History.

THE BEGINNINGS

The Birth of History

It all started in a previous class, Design Studio, where the idea was concepted, graphics were made, and the beginings of the story were first written down. As I went through the program, I dug more into AR capabilities and got more comfortable with javascript, and saw how I might be able to apply those learnings to this kooky idea about overlaying history with our fair city.

RESEARCH

Getting into AR.js and A-Frame

Using some of the groundwork set up from previous courses, I attempted to teach myself the javascript languages AR.js and A-Frame. AR.js is relatively new, but fortunately there was some good tutorials to at least get my head aroun it. A-Frame has been around for a while, but is mainly used for VR applications, but it combines with AR.js and you have to know a little about both to make things show up in your mobile camera.

PROCESS

Bending the code to my will

Using some of the groundwork set up from previous courses, I set out to make a working prototype of the basic augmented reality object, the photo of the whale, and worked through hours of tutuorials and feeds to get it to successfully load in the camera view.

THE TALLEST OF TALES

The Stories

Once I had the basic functionality down and assets created, I began to write, edit, and rewrite the stories. Then I recorded audio for each experience and mixed in music and sound as appropriate, so once the user was at the historic site, the story could be listened to within the AR experience. Granted, they do have to suffer through my silly narration but hopefully the sound of their laughter fills in over the plot holes.
